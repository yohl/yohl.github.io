I like to think that I'm a UX designer trapped in a programmer's body. My technical background does help me understand and empathize with the engineers that I work with, and it helps me to see what's programmatically achievable and what's beyond the realm of the system. However, I believe I'm a UXer at heart. I love seeing different design challenges and the approach needed to come up with various solutions. I absolutely love to think in the shoes of my users. It's such a unique way of thinking, because you have to give up your own tendancies to make the right decisions.
